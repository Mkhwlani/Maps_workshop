# 3D Visualization With Real-World Data

**Inspired by:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rXvU7bPJ8n4

---

## The Premise

The premise is basically to have the students learn 3D visualization techniques and softwares — such as useful tools like Google 3D tiles, and CesiumJS to learn to work with geospatial data.

Many industries use such data, and many firms today like Palantir have their entire business model on turning real world data into useful clear visualizations that can be used for business, military, environmental and social analysis. Today data is more available and important than ever, and this workshop can provide students with a foundation in using real world data for real use cases.

The workshop touches topics such as 3D hologram softwares, holographic world maps, and then given access to real world data such as flight or weather data to integrate with their visualization.

## Final Day Competition

The final day would focus on having the students decide what to do, with a competition. We would help or answer questions, and provide further resources but the students decide their own final visualization. Students will be encouraged to create something that could offer real value; perhaps a weather tracker, or a traffic map of Riyadh — or more useful, something that we don't already have on the apps on our phones like a flight tracker or live camera feeds of different cities when you zoom in.

## Workshop Schedule

### Day 1 — Introduction & Setup

Students will come in and be introduced to React, using javascript with python to create a 3D visualization using Google 3D tiles. CesiumJS will be used alongside it, and students will be basically told what to do and how to create a starter map.

Like the first workshop, for this day, we will provide them with most of the code, but the students have to complete some last steps. For example they will get all the help to get started and get going, but eventually they will be asked to use their skills — and we will show them our example to compare.

By the end of day 1 (which could be fused with day 2 with a break), we basically cover their basics in using React, get them set up, and have them start running our code, before making final changes to it at the end.

We will provide a github repo with all the starter code, they will only have to make minimal changes.

> Day 1 may have to leak into Day 2 depending on how fast students have everything setup and are able to run and test their websites.

### Day 2 — Real-World Data Integration

Then, we want students to be able to actually use flight data, and show them how it can be overlaid on the visualization. This introduces them not only to making real holographic visualizations of the globe (day 1), they combined it with real world data to make a useful product.

> Day 2 may be much shorter than Day 1, so it may be best to do them the same day on a Thursday.

### Day 3 — Competition Day

Competition day, where the students are given examples of what may be good products that exist today (Google maps for traffic, weather app for precipitation data, or Waze which has all speeding cameras on it).

We then tell them we want them to use the base they have now (just the world and flights), forget about flights or expand on it if they wish, and try to make a useful product that utilizes real world data.

There are many off the top of our head but that are not novel — the challenge is for students to make something new.

## Judging Criteria

As judges we would score on **quality** and **novelty**. Because someone with something completely new and useful, with 10/10 on novelty may have a 8/10 in quality, so incentivizing creativity helps students especially those who might abuse AI. We can adjust and solely focus on creativity or see as we go.

**Ultimately a winner should be creative, use interesting data, and make something useful for someone.**
